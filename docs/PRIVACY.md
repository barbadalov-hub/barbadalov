# Privacy Policy — Lumo

_Last updated: 2026-07-14_

Lumo ("the app") is a personal life-tracking app (finance, health, food,
habits, goals, mood and an AI assistant). This policy explains what happens to
your data. In short: **your data stays on your device**, and the app works fully
offline.

## What data the app handles

You enter this data yourself — transactions, meals, health metrics, habits,
goals, mood notes, etc. The app also derives insights from it locally.

## Where your data is stored

- **On your device only, by default.** Entries are saved in local storage
  (a JSON file on desktop/mobile, `localStorage` in the browser/PWA). Nothing is
  sent to a server unless you explicitly enable cloud sync.

## What the app does **not** do

- No advertising.
- No third-party analytics, tracking, or profiling.
- No selling or sharing of your data.

## Camera

The camera is used **only** for on-device receipt scanning (OCR) to read prices
from a photo. Images are processed **on your device** and are **not uploaded**.
You can use the app without granting camera access.

## Optional cloud sync

Cloud sync is **off by default**. If you turn it on and sign in, your data is
stored in **your own** cloud project (e.g. your Firebase project) so you can
access it across devices. You can turn it off at any time.

## Notifications

On phones, the app may send local reminders/alerts you configure. These are
generated on-device; no data leaves the device to produce them.

## Your control over your data

- **Export:** More → Backup lets you export all your data to a file.
- **Delete:** you can erase your data from the app at any time; uninstalling the
  app removes locally stored data.

## Children

The app is not directed at children under 13.

We may update this policy; the "Last updated" date above will change
